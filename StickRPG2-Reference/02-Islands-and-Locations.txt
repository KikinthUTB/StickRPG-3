# Stick RPG 2 - Islands and Locations

## Island Overview
Stick RPG 2 features multiple islands that unlock as the player progresses and meets certain requirements.

## Main Island (Starting Island)

### Residential Areas
- **Park Bench** - Starting location, where player wakes up
- **Apartment Complex** - Low-cost housing option ($500/day rent)
- **Trailer Park** - Cheap alternative housing
- **2D Suburbs** - Mid-tier housing area
- **Penthouse** - High-end housing (requires high income and stats)

### Commercial District
- **Convenience Store** - Buy basic items, energy drinks, food
- **Gun Store** - Purchase weapons (requires license)
- **Car Dealership** - Buy vehicles for faster travel
- **Furniture Store** - Buy items to furnish your living space
- **Electronics Store** - Computers, phones, and gadgets
- **Clothing Store** - Outfits that affect charm and appearance

### Work Locations
- **McDonald's Parody (McStick's)** - Entry-level job
- **Convenience Store** - Cashier position
- **Mall** - Various retail positions
- **Construction Site** - Manual labor, requires strength
- **Office Building** - Corporate jobs, requires intelligence
- **Law Firm** - High-paying legal work, high intelligence required
- **Stock Market** - Trading floor, very high requirements

### Educational
- **University** - Study to increase intelligence
- **Library** - Free but slower intelligence gain
- **2D Gym** - Work out to increase strength
- **Fight Club** - Illegal fighting for money and strength

### Entertainment
- **Bar/Pub** - Socialize, drink, play darts
- **Casino** - Gambling mini-games (Blackjack, Slots, Poker)
- **Movie Theater** - Watch films, dates
- **Nightclub** - Late-night activities, meet NPCs
- **Skateboard Park** - Recreation area
- **Basketball Court** - Sports activities

### Special Locations
- **Bank** - Deposit/withdraw money, get loans
- **Hospital** - Heal injuries, increase max health
- **Police Station** - Pay fines, bail, get gun license
- **City Hall** - Government services, marriage license

## The Second Island

### Unlocking Requirements
- Intelligence: 300+
- Charm: 200+
- Money: $5,000 for bridge toll or season pass

### Key Locations
- **Beach Area** - Recreational zone
- **Seafood Restaurant** - High-end dining and job opportunity
- **Yacht Club** - Exclusive social venue
- **Marine Biology Lab** - Scientific jobs
- **Surf Shop** - Beach equipment and clothing
- **Beach House** - Premium housing option
- **Tiki Bar** - Tropical themed entertainment
- **Lighthouse** - Scenic location, contains story elements

### Story Missions on Second Island
- Professor Ansel's Lab (dimensional research)
- Beach Cleanup Mission
- Yacht Race Event
- Coral Reef Investigation

## The Third Island (Industrial Island)

### Unlocking Requirements
- Strength: 400+
- Intelligence: 350+
- Completion of certain story missions

### Key Locations
- **Factory Complex** - Manufacturing jobs
- **Warehouse District** - Storage and logistics work
- **Chemical Plant** - Dangerous but high-paying work
- **Industrial Gym** - Advanced strength training
- **Worker's Union Hall** - Special job opportunities
- **Scrapyard** - Find/sell items
- **Power Plant** - Technical positions
- **Shipping Docks** - Import/export jobs

### Story Missions on Third Island
- Factory Safety Investigation
- Chemical Spill Response
- Union Negotiation Quest
- Industrial Sabotage Mystery

## The Fourth Island (Corporate/Executive Island)

### Unlocking Requirements
- All stats: 500+
- Net worth: $50,000+
- Corporate reputation

### Key Locations
- **Corporate Headquarters** - CEO and executive positions
- **Executive Club** - Ultra-exclusive socializing
- **Private Airport** - Fast travel between islands
- **Penthouse Suites** - Ultimate housing
- **Stock Exchange** - Advanced trading
- **Executive Golf Course** - Networking and deals
- **Five-Star Restaurant** - Luxury dining
- **Private Hospital** - Premium healthcare

### Story Missions on Fourth Island
- Corporate Takeover Quest
- Insider Trading Investigation
- Board Meeting Challenges
- Director Confrontation (Final Story Mission)

## Hidden/Secret Locations

### The Z Island (Secret Island)
- **Unlocking**: Hidden requirements, mysterious clues
- **Location**: Contains end-game challenges
- **Features**: 
  - Special training facilities
  - Unique items and weapons
  - Dimensional portal experiments
  - Secret boss encounters

### Underground Locations
- **Subway System** - Fast travel network
- **Sewer Tunnels** - Hidden passages between islands
- **Secret Fight Club** - Underground combat
- **Black Market** - Illegal goods and services

### Seasonal/Time-Based Locations
- **Rooftop Parties** - Night-time only
- **Secret Beach** - Accessible during specific conditions
- **Abandoned Warehouse** - Mission-specific location

## Mission Locations Reference

### Early Missions (Main Island)
- "First Day" - Park Bench, Apartment Complex
- "Get a Job" - Various work locations
- "Study Hard" - Library, University
- "Make Friends" - Bar, Nightclub

### Mid Missions (Multiple Islands)
- "Island Hopping" - Bridge between islands
- "Corporate Ladder" - Office buildings, Corporate Island
- "Beach Party" - Second Island beach
- "Factory Blues" - Industrial Island

### Late Missions (All Islands)
- "The Director's Plan" - Corporate Island HQ
- "Dimensional Secrets" - Professor Ansel's Lab
- "Ultimate Challenge" - Z Island
- "Coming Home" - Final mission locations

## Transportation Between Islands

### Methods
- **Walking** - Free, slow, only nearby areas
- **Skateboard** - Faster than walking, unlocked early
- **Bicycle** - Moderate speed, good stamina
- **Car** - Fast travel, requires purchase and insurance
- **Bus** - Public transit, cheap, moderate speed
- **Taxi** - Quick but expensive
- **Helicopter** - Late game, very fast, very expensive
- **Bridge Tolls** - Pay per crossing or buy season pass

## Notes for Stick RPG 3
- Progressive island unlocking creates good progression curve
- Mix of required stats and story progression for access worked well
- Fast travel options become important as world expands
- Each island having distinct theme and opportunities prevents monotony
- Hidden locations add exploration value
- Consider making more locations interactive with unique mini-games
- More dynamic events in locations (time-based, random encounters)
